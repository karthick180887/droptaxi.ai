Airport Taxi Guide South India: Pickup and Drop Service for All Major Airports

Landing at a South Indian airport and need a reliable taxi to your destination? Whether you are flying into Chennai for business, arriving at Coimbatore to head to the Nilgiris, or landing at Cochin for a Kerala trip, this guide covers everything you need to know about airport taxi services at the 10 busiest airports in South India.

For each airport, we cover the IATA code, distance to the city center, typical taxi fare ranges, available transport options, and practical tips for a smooth transfer. DropTaxi provides one-way airport taxi service from all these airports, meaning you can book a pickup directly to any outstation destination without paying return fare.

1. Chennai International Airport (MAA)

IATA Code: MAA | Location: Tirusulam, South Chennai | Distance to City Center: 18 km (T. Nagar) | Drive Time to City: 30 to 75 minutes depending on traffic

Chennai Airport is the fourth-busiest airport in India and the primary gateway to Tamil Nadu. It has two terminals -- the domestic terminal (Terminal 1) and the international terminal (Terminal 4, formerly Terminal 3). The airport is well-connected to the city via the Chennai Metro Rail (Airport station on the Blue Line) and suburban rail (Tirusulam station).

Taxi options at the airport: Pre-paid taxi counter operated by the government is available in the arrivals hall. App-based cabs (Ola, Uber) with a dedicated pickup zone. DropTaxi for outstation one-way drops to cities like Vellore, Pondicherry, Trichy, and beyond.

Fare estimates: Airport to T. Nagar: INR 400-600 (sedan). Airport to Vellore: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Pondicherry: INR 3,000-4,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Bangalore: INR 5,000-7,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: Exit from the arrivals level and look for the pre-paid taxi booth if you want a fixed-rate city transfer. For outstation trips, pre-booking with DropTaxi ensures your driver is waiting when you land, with a name board at arrivals. Traffic on GST Road (between the airport and city) is heaviest between 8-10 AM and 5-8 PM -- allow extra time during these windows. The Chennai Airport Metro station connects directly to the terminal and is the fastest way to reach Central Chennai during peak traffic.

2. Kempegowda International Airport, Bangalore (BLR)

IATA Code: BLR | Location: Devanahalli, North Bangalore | Distance to City Center: 38 km (MG Road) | Drive Time to City: 60 to 120 minutes depending on traffic

Bangalore Airport is the third-busiest airport in India, handling over 35 million passengers annually. Located in Devanahalli, about 38 km north of the city center, it is significantly farther from the city than most other South Indian airports. This distance makes pre-booked taxi services particularly valuable for travelers heading to outstation destinations in Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, or Kerala.

Taxi options at the airport: KSTDC (Karnataka State Tourism) pre-paid taxi counter. App-based cabs with a designated pickup area at the ground level of the multi-level car park. Airport taxi service operated by BMTC (Vayu Vajra bus service to major city points). DropTaxi for direct outstation drops to Mysore, Coorg, Chennai, Ooty, and other destinations.

Fare estimates: Airport to MG Road: INR 700-1,200 (sedan). Airport to Mysore: INR 3,500-5,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Hosur: INR 1,500-2,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Coorg: INR 6,000-8,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: The airport toll (currently around INR 110) is charged at the Devanahalli toll plaza and is paid by the passenger in most services. Night charges (usually 10-15% extra) apply for pickups between 10 PM and 6 AM. The NICE Ring Road provides a faster route to South Bangalore, Mysore Road, and Hosur Road -- ask your driver to take it during peak hours. Terminal 2 (international) opened recently, so confirm which terminal your flight uses before arranging pickup.

3. Coimbatore International Airport (CJB)

IATA Code: CJB | Location: Peelamedu, Central Coimbatore | Distance to City Center: 10 km (Gandhipuram) | Drive Time to City: 20 to 40 minutes

Coimbatore Airport is the second-busiest airport in Tamil Nadu and a key gateway to the Nilgiri hill stations (Ooty, Coonoor, Kotagiri), Palani Hills (Kodaikanal via Palani), and Kerala destinations (Munnar, Palakkad). Its central location within the city makes city transfers quick, but the real value of the airport lies in its proximity to mountain destinations.

Fare estimates: Airport to Gandhipuram: INR 200-400 (sedan). Airport to Ooty: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Coonoor: INR 2,000-3,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Munnar: INR 4,500-6,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Palakkad: INR 1,500-2,500 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: If heading to Ooty or Coonoor, depart from the airport before noon to ensure you complete the ghat road section in daylight. The Mettupalayam-Ooty ghat road has 36 hairpin bends and is not recommended for night driving. For Munnar, the route via Udumalpet and Marayoor is more scenic than the Pollachi route.

4. Madurai Airport (IXM)

IATA Code: IXM | Location: Avaniapuram, Madurai | Distance to City Center: 12 km (Meenakshi Temple) | Drive Time to City: 20 to 35 minutes

Madurai Airport serves as the gateway to southern Tamil Nadu's cultural heartland. It is a relatively small airport with domestic flights connecting to Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Delhi. The airport's main value is its proximity to the Meenakshi Temple, Kodaikanal hill station, and Rameshwaram pilgrimage town.

Fare estimates: Airport to Meenakshi Temple: INR 250-400 (sedan). Airport to Kodaikanal: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Rameshwaram: INR 3,000-4,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Trichy: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: If heading to Kodaikanal, allow at least 3 hours for the drive including the 23-hairpin ghat section from Batlagundu. For Rameshwaram, the route via Sivaganga and Ramanathapuram takes about 3.5 hours. Pre-booking is especially important for early morning flights as auto-rickshaws and app cabs may not be readily available before 6 AM.

5. Tiruchirappalli International Airport (TRZ)

IATA Code: TRZ | Location: Civil Aerodrome, Trichy | Distance to City Center: 6 km (Trichy Junction) | Drive Time to City: 15 to 25 minutes

Trichy Airport is one of the few airports in Tamil Nadu with international connectivity (flights to Singapore, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, and the Middle East). It serves as a convenient entry point for the Chola temple circuit (Thanjavur, Kumbakonam, Chidambaram) and the central Tamil Nadu region.

Fare estimates: Airport to Trichy Junction: INR 150-300 (sedan). Airport to Srirangam Temple: INR 200-350 (sedan). Airport to Thanjavur: INR 1,200-2,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Kumbakonam: INR 1,500-2,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Madurai: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: Trichy Airport is very close to the city, making it one of the most convenient airports in South India for city transfers. For temple circuit travelers, Trichy is the ideal airport to fly into -- Srirangam, Thanjavur, and Kumbakonam are all within 1.5 hours by taxi. International passengers should clear customs early as the international terminal is small and can get crowded during multiple flight arrivals.

6. Cochin International Airport (COK)

IATA Code: COK | Location: Nedumbassery, Ernakulam District | Distance to City Center: 28 km (Ernakulam/Fort Kochi) | Drive Time to City: 45 to 75 minutes

Cochin Airport is the busiest airport in Kerala and the first airport in the world to be fully powered by solar energy. It serves as the primary gateway to Kerala's tourism destinations -- backwaters, beaches, hill stations, and Ayurvedic resorts. The airport handles significant international traffic, especially from the Middle East, due to Kerala's large diaspora.

Fare estimates: Airport to Fort Kochi: INR 800-1,200 (sedan). Airport to Alleppey (Alappuzha): INR 1,500-2,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Munnar: INR 3,000-4,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Thekkady: INR 3,500-5,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Trivandrum: INR 5,000-7,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: The airport has two international terminals and one domestic terminal. Check your terminal before arranging pickup. For Munnar-bound travelers, leave the airport before noon to complete the mountain drive in daylight. The Alappuzha (Alleppey) houseboat jetties are about 1.5 hours from the airport -- ideal for a same-day transfer to start your backwater cruise.

7. Rajiv Gandhi International Airport, Hyderabad (HYD)

IATA Code: HYD | Location: Shamshabad, South Hyderabad | Distance to City Center: 24 km (Hitech City) to 30 km (Secunderabad) | Drive Time to City: 35 to 75 minutes

Hyderabad Airport is one of the best-designed airports in India and a major hub for domestic and international travel. Located in Shamshabad on the city's southern outskirts, it is the gateway to Telangana and northern Andhra Pradesh. The airport serves as a transit point for travelers heading to Vijayawada, Tirupati, and other destinations in the region.

Fare estimates: Airport to Hitech City: INR 600-1,000 (sedan). Airport to Secunderabad: INR 700-1,200 (sedan). Airport to Vijayawada: INR 4,500-6,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Warangal: INR 3,000-4,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Tirupati: INR 8,000-11,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: The Nehru Outer Ring Road (ORR) provides fast access to most parts of Hyderabad from the airport. Traffic on the ORR is usually manageable, but the exits to Hitech City and Gachibowli can be congested during IT sector shift changes (9-10 AM, 6-8 PM). Hyderabad Metro does not directly connect to the airport yet, so road transfer remains the only option for most travelers.

8. Mangalore International Airport (IXE)

IATA Code: IXE | Location: Bajpe, Mangalore | Distance to City Center: 13 km | Drive Time to City: 25 to 40 minutes

Mangalore Airport sits on a tabletop runway atop a hilltop plateau, making it one of the most dramatically positioned airports in India. It serves as a gateway to Dakshina Kannada, Udupi, and the Karnataka coast, with connections to the Middle East, Bangalore, Mumbai, and other domestic cities.

Fare estimates: Airport to Mangalore City: INR 300-500 (sedan). Airport to Udupi: INR 1,200-2,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Coorg (Madikeri): INR 4,000-6,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Bangalore: INR 6,500-9,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: The tabletop runway means flights can be delayed or diverted during heavy monsoon rain (June-August). Plan buffer time for connections. The road from the airport to the city descends from the plateau and has several curves -- nothing dangerous but worth noting for first-time visitors. For Coorg-bound travelers, the route via Sullia takes about 4 hours and passes through scenic plantation country.

9. Trivandrum International Airport (TRV)

IATA Code: TRV | Location: Chakai, Thiruvananthapuram | Distance to City Center: 6 km (Central Station) | Drive Time to City: 15 to 30 minutes

Trivandrum Airport is Kerala's second international airport and the gateway to South Kerala's beaches, backwaters, and the Western Ghats. Its proximity to the city center makes it one of the most convenient airports in South India. The airport handles significant Middle Eastern traffic and is a popular entry point for Kovalam Beach, Varkala, and Kanyakumari.

Fare estimates: Airport to Trivandrum Central: INR 200-350 (sedan). Airport to Kovalam Beach: INR 300-500 (sedan). Airport to Varkala: INR 1,200-2,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Kanyakumari: INR 1,800-3,000 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Alleppey: INR 3,500-5,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: Kovalam Beach is just 15 km from the airport, making it one of the most accessible beach destinations from any airport in India. For Kanyakumari, the drive takes about 2 hours via NH 66 through Nagercoil. Trivandrum is also the starting point for the classic Kerala backwater route (Trivandrum to Alleppey to Kochi).

10. Tirupati Airport (TIR)

IATA Code: TIR | Location: Renigunta, Tirupati | Distance to City Center: 14 km (Tirupati town) | Drive Time to City: 20 to 35 minutes | Distance to Tirumala: 28 km from airport

Tirupati Airport primarily serves pilgrims visiting the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple (Balaji Temple), the richest and most-visited Hindu temple in the world. The airport has direct flights from Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Delhi, and other cities. It is a small but well-functioning airport that handles increasing traffic due to the temple's enormous draw.

Fare estimates: Airport to Tirupati Town: INR 300-500 (sedan). Airport to Tirumala (via the ghat road): INR 600-1,000 (sedan). Airport to Chennai: INR 2,500-3,500 (sedan, one-way). Airport to Bangalore: INR 3,500-5,000 (sedan, one-way).

Tips: The ghat road from Tirupati to Tirumala has strict vehicle regulations. Private vehicles may be restricted during peak pilgrimage days, so check with the TTD (Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams) website before planning. If the ghat road is restricted, free bus services run from Tirupati to Tirumala. For same-day darshan, arriving on an early morning flight and heading directly to Tirumala maximizes your chance of completing the visit in one day. After darshan, book a one-way taxi to Chennai or Bangalore instead of waiting for a return flight.
