South India Hill Stations by Taxi: Complete Travel Guide

The Western and Eastern Ghats of South India cradle some of the most beautiful hill stations on the subcontinent. From the misty tea estates of Munnar to the coffee plantations of Coorg, from the colonial charm of Ooty to the unspoiled tranquility of Valparai, these highland retreats offer cool weather, stunning vistas, and a welcome escape from the plains below.

This guide covers the 10 best hill stations in South India -- spanning Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka -- with practical information on altitude, weather, attractions, how to get there, and why a one-way taxi is often the smartest way to reach and leave these mountain destinations.

1. Ooty (Udhagamandalam), Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 2,240 meters | Best Season: April to June, October to November | Nearest Airport: Coimbatore (88 km) | Nearest Major City: Coimbatore

Ooty, the "Queen of Hill Stations," sits atop the Nilgiri Hills in the Western Ghats. Established as a summer retreat by the British East India Company in the early 19th century, it retains its colonial heritage in the form of stone churches, botanical gardens, and the famous Nilgiri Mountain Railway -- a UNESCO World Heritage rack railway that climbs from Mettupalayam through 208 curves and 16 tunnels.

Top attractions: Ooty Botanical Gardens, originally laid out in 1848 with a fossilized tree trunk over 20 million years old. Ooty Lake for boating. Doddabetta Peak at 2,637 meters, the highest point in the Nilgiris. Rose Garden with over 20,000 varieties. Tea factory visits at the Nilgiri Tea Museum. Pykara Falls and Lake. Avalanche Lake for a wilderness experience (permit required). The scenic Nilgiri Mountain Railway toy train ride.

Getting there by taxi: The most common route is from Coimbatore via Mettupalayam, climbing the 36 hairpin bends of the Nilgiri Ghat road. From Bangalore, the drive takes about 6 hours via Mysore and Bandipur. From Mysore, it is about 3 hours via the Bandipur-Mudumalai forest corridor.

2. Kodaikanal, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 2,133 meters | Best Season: April to June, September to November | Nearest Airport: Madurai (120 km) | Nearest Major City: Madurai

Known as the "Princess of Hill Stations," Kodaikanal sits in the Palani Hills and is built around a beautiful star-shaped lake. Unlike Ooty, which was developed by the British as an administrative center, Kodaikanal was established by American missionaries in 1845 and retains a more intimate, less commercialized character. The town is surrounded by dense shola forests, grasslands, and ancient granite outcrops.

Top attractions: Kodaikanal Lake for boating, cycling, and horse riding along the lakeside path. Coaker's Walk, a 1-kilometer paved path along the southern slope with views extending to the Vaigai Dam and Madurai plains on clear days. Pillar Rocks, three massive granite pillars rising 400 feet from a forested chasm. Bryant Park, a 20-acre botanical garden. Pine Forest with towering pines planted in the 1900s. Bear Shola Falls (best during monsoon). Dolphin's Nose viewpoint. Berijam Lake, a pristine reservoir inside a protected forest (permit from Forest Department required). Kurinji Andavar Temple, where the kurinji flower blooms once every 12 years.

Getting there by taxi: From Madurai, the drive takes about 3 hours via Batlagundu, climbing through 23 hairpin bends. From Coimbatore, it is about 5 hours via Palani. From Chennai, the most common route goes via Trichy and Dindigul (8-9 hours).

3. Munnar, Kerala

Altitude: 1,532 meters | Best Season: September to May (December to February for the best weather) | Nearest Airport: Cochin (COK, 110 km) | Nearest Major City: Kochi

Munnar is the jewel of Kerala's highlands, a vast landscape of rolling tea estates that stretch to the horizon in every shade of green. The name "Munnar" comes from "Moonu Aar" (three rivers), referring to the confluence of the Madhurapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundaly rivers. Once the summer resort of the British Raj government in South India, Munnar is today Kerala's most visited hill station.

Top attractions: Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and the site of the spectacular Neelakurinji bloom (once every 12 years, next in 2030). Top Station, the highest point on the Munnar-Kodaikanal road with views of Tamil Nadu's Western Ghats. Mattupetty Dam and Lake. Echo Point. Tea Museum tracing the history of tea cultivation in Munnar since the 1880s. Lakkam Waterfall. Anamudi Peak (2,695 meters), the highest peak in South India. Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary for thorn-scrub forest ecosystem.

Getting there by taxi: From Kochi, it is 4 to 4.5 hours via Adimali. From Coimbatore, the drive is about 5 hours via Udumalpet and Marayoor. From Madurai, it is approximately 4.5 hours via Theni and Bodinayakanur.

4. Wayanad, Kerala

Altitude: 700 to 2,100 meters | Best Season: October to May | Nearest Airport: Calicut (Kozhikode, 100 km) | Nearest Major City: Kozhikode

Wayanad is not a single hill station but an entire highland district of Kerala, nestled within the Western Ghats with a remarkable diversity of landscapes -- from dense tropical forests and bamboo groves to spice plantations, caves, and ancient rock shelters. The name comes from "Vayal Nadu" (land of paddy fields), and the district still retains a deep agricultural character alongside its natural beauty.

Top attractions: Edakkal Caves, prehistoric rock shelters with petroglyphs dating back 6,000 years. Banasura Sagar Dam, the largest earth dam in India. Chembra Peak (2,100 meters) with a heart-shaped lake near the summit. Meenmutty Falls, a three-tiered waterfall requiring a short trek. Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary (part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve) for elephants and bison. Kuruva Island, a river delta with dense bamboo thickets. Pookode Lake surrounded by evergreen forest. Jain Temple at Sulthan Bathery dating to the 13th century.

Getting there by taxi: From Bangalore, it is about 6 hours via Mysore and Gundlupet. From Kozhikode, the drive is just 2.5 hours via the Thamarassery Ghat pass with its 9 hairpin bends. From Mysore, it is about 3 hours via the Bandipur-Sulthan Bathery road.

5. Coonoor, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 1,850 meters | Best Season: October to March | Nearest Airport: Coimbatore (70 km) | Nearest Major City: Coimbatore

Coonoor is the quieter, more refined neighbor of Ooty in the Nilgiris. While Ooty attracts the crowds, Coonoor draws those who prefer tea estate walks, colonial-era bungalows, and misty morning views without the tourist bustle. It is the second-largest hill station in the Nilgiris and has been gaining a reputation as a food and cafe destination, with excellent bakeries and restaurants run by local residents and city transplants.

Top attractions: Sim's Park, a stunning 12-hectare botanical garden with over 1,000 plant species set in a natural ravine. Dolphin's Nose viewpoint, offering one of the most dramatic views in the Nilgiris -- a sheer drop with Catherine Falls visible in the distance. Lamb's Rock for panoramic views of the Coimbatore plains. Hidden Valley for quiet forest walks. Highfield Tea Factory for understanding how Nilgiri tea is processed from leaf to cup. Droog Fort, a Tipu Sultan-era fortification accessible by a short trek through tea estates. The Nilgiri Mountain Railway runs through Coonoor, and the Coonoor-Ooty stretch is the most scenic segment.

Getting there by taxi: From Coimbatore, it is about 1.5 hours via Mettupalayam. From Ooty, it is just 30 minutes downhill. Coonoor can be combined with an Ooty trip or visited independently for a quieter experience.

6. Coorg (Kodagu), Karnataka

Altitude: 900 to 1,750 meters | Best Season: October to March (monsoon June-September for waterfall lovers) | Nearest Airport: Mysore (120 km) or Mangalore (150 km) | Nearest Major City: Mysore

Coorg is Karnataka's premier hill district, famous for its coffee plantations, martial culture, and misty mountain landscapes. Unlike most hill stations that center on a single town, Coorg is an entire district (Kodagu) with the main town of Madikeri serving as the hub. The Kodava people of Coorg have a distinctive culture, cuisine, and identity that sets this region apart from anywhere else in South India.

Top attractions: Abbey Falls, a scenic waterfall surrounded by coffee and spice plantations. Raja's Seat in Madikeri, a garden viewpoint where Kodagu kings once watched sunsets. Talakaveri, the origin of the River Cauvery at the top of Brahmagiri Hill. Namdroling Monastery at Bylakuppe, the largest Tibetan settlement in South India with a stunning golden temple. Dubare Elephant Camp on the banks of the Cauvery for elephant interaction. Iruppu Falls near the Brahmagiri range. Coffee plantation stays and tours -- Coorg produces some of India's finest arabica and robusta beans. Mandalpatti Peak for a jeep ride and panoramic views above the clouds.

Getting there by taxi: From Bangalore, it is about 5.5 hours via Mysore. From Mysore, it is 3 hours. From Mangalore, it is about 3.5 hours via Sullia.

7. Yercaud, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 1,515 meters | Best Season: Year-round (September to May is ideal) | Nearest Airport: Trichy (185 km) or Salem (35 km by road, no airport) | Nearest Major City: Salem

Yercaud is a compact, affordable hill station in the Shevaroy Hills of the Eastern Ghats, often called the "Jewel of the South" or the "Poor Man's Ooty." Its proximity to Salem (just 35 km) makes it one of the most accessible hill stations in Tamil Nadu. The 20 hairpin bends driving up from Salem are scenic but not terrifying, and the town itself has a peaceful, village-like atmosphere with coffee estates, orange orchards, and a pleasant lake.

Top attractions: Yercaud Lake (Emerald Lake) for boating and lakeside walks. Lady's Seat and Gent's Seat viewpoints with panoramic views of the Salem plains. Kiliyur Falls, a 90-meter cascade reached by a trek of about 2 kilometers. Bear's Cave (Karadu Cave), a small natural cave formation. Pagoda Point for sunset views. Botanical Garden and orchidarium. Shevaroy Temple at the highest point of the hills. Coffee and spice plantations open for tours. Anna Park, an amusement area near the lake.

Getting there by taxi: From Salem, it is just 1 hour uphill. From Chennai, it is about 5.5 to 6 hours via the Salem bypass. From Trichy, it is about 3.5 hours via Namakkal.

8. Yelagiri, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 940 meters | Best Season: Year-round (October to February is best) | Nearest Airport: Bangalore (160 km) | Nearest Major City: Vellore (90 km)

Yelagiri is a small, quiet hill station in the Vellore district that remains blissfully under-the-radar compared to Ooty or Kodaikanal. Sitting at a modest altitude of 940 meters, it does not get as cold as the higher Western Ghat stations, but it offers pleasant weather, scenic views, and a genuine escape from city noise. The 14 hairpin bends driving up from Jolarpet add a touch of adventure to the approach.

Top attractions: Punganoor Lake (Yelagiri Lake) for boating and lakeside relaxation. Nature Park with a rose garden, children's play area, and musical fountain. Swamimalai Hills for trekking (the highest point in the Yelagiri range at 1,410 meters). Jalagamparai Waterfalls, a seasonal waterfall accessible by a short trek. Paragliding at the Yelagiri Adventure Zone (seasonal). Nilavoor Lake, a quieter lake for picnics. Telescope observatory for stargazing on clear nights. Summer Festival held annually in May with cultural events and flower shows.

Getting there by taxi: From Chennai, it is about 4 hours via the Vellore-Jolarpet route. From Bangalore, it is about 3.5 hours via Hosur and Krishnagiri. From Vellore, it is 2 hours via Vaniyambadi.

9. Valparai, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 1,196 meters | Best Season: December to May (monsoon is extremely wet) | Nearest Airport: Coimbatore (100 km) | Nearest Major City: Coimbatore

Valparai is one of the most scenic yet least commercialized hill stations in Tamil Nadu. Located in the Anaimalai Hills of the Western Ghats, this small town is surrounded by tea and coffee estates, dense rainforests, and grasslands that form part of the Anaimalai Tiger Reserve. The drive from Pollachi to Valparai involves 40 hairpin bends through the Aliyar-Valparai Ghat road, passing through pristine forest where sightings of elephants, lion-tailed macaques, and hornbills are common.

Top attractions: Sholayar Dam, one of the tallest dams in Tamil Nadu with stunning views. Monkey Falls, a popular bathing spot at the base of the ghat road. Nallamudi Poonjolai, a pristine hilltop garden above the clouds with panoramic views. Balaji Temple viewpoint. Grass Hills, a vast expanse of rolling grassland within the Anaimalai Tiger Reserve (permit needed). Iyerpadi and Athirapilly-style waterfalls in the surrounding forest. Lion-tailed macaque sighting areas along the ghat road. Tea estate walks through working plantations.

Getting there by taxi: From Coimbatore, it is about 3.5 hours via Pollachi and the 40-hairpin ghat road. From Madurai, it is about 5 hours. The ghat road requires an experienced driver comfortable with mountain driving.

10. Kotagiri, Tamil Nadu

Altitude: 1,793 meters | Best Season: Year-round (October to March is ideal) | Nearest Airport: Coimbatore (95 km) | Nearest Major City: Coimbatore

Kotagiri is the oldest hill station in the Nilgiris and arguably the least touristy of the three main Nilgiri towns (Ooty, Coonoor, and Kotagiri). It was the first choice of the British for a hill settlement before Ooty was developed, and it retains an unhurried, colonial-era calm that Ooty has long since lost. The town is home to several indigenous Toda tribal settlements and offers some of the most pristine trekking trails in the Nilgiris.

Top attractions: Catherine Falls, one of the tallest waterfalls in the Nilgiris at 76 meters (best viewed from Dolphin's Nose in Coonoor, but accessible by trek from Kotagiri). Kodanad Viewpoint with sweeping views of the Moyar Valley and the Mysore Plateau far below. Longwood Shola, one of the last surviving patches of tropical montane cloud forest in the Nilgiris. Rangaswamy Peak and Pillar for trekking. Toda tribal hamlets (visit with respect and local guide). Sullivan Memorial, commemorating John Sullivan who established the first European settlement in the Nilgiris. Tea and coffee estate walks through plantations that have been producing for over a century.

Getting there by taxi: From Coimbatore, it is about 2 hours via Mettupalayam. From Ooty, it is about 1 hour. Kotagiri can be visited as a day trip from Ooty or Coonoor, or as a standalone destination for those seeking genuine solitude.

Why Take a Taxi to Hill Stations?

Hill stations present unique travel challenges that make a taxi the most practical option for most travelers.

  • Ghat road driving is demanding. Routes to most hill stations involve dozens of hairpin bends on narrow mountain roads. Driving these roads for the first time, in unfamiliar vehicles, is stressful and potentially dangerous -- especially during monsoon or at night. A professional driver who knows the ghat roads well makes the journey safer and more enjoyable.
  • No direct public transport. Most hill stations require bus changes at foothill towns. Direct bus services are infrequent, uncomfortable for long journeys, and do not allow flexibility in timing or stops.
  • One-way pricing makes sense. With DropTaxi, you pay only for the one-way distance. Take a taxi up to the hill station, enjoy your stay for as many days as you want, and then book another one-way taxi back or onward to your next destination. No return fare charges.
  • Local sightseeing is easier. Hill station attractions are often spread across a wide area connected by winding roads. Having a driver for your transit legs means you arrive rested and ready to explore -- either on foot, by local auto, or by booking a local sightseeing vehicle.
  • Luggage comfort. Buses on ghat roads stack luggage on rooftops. A taxi keeps your bags secure in the boot, with climate control and comfortable seating for the entire climb.

Packing Tips for South India Hill Stations

  • Layers are essential. Temperatures can drop to 5-10 degrees Celsius at night in Ooty, Kodaikanal, and Munnar during winter. Carry a warm jacket, fleece, or shawl even if the plains are hot.
  • Rain gear year-round. The Western Ghats receive both southwest (June-September) and northeast (October-December) monsoons. A compact rain jacket or umbrella is advisable in any season.
  • Comfortable walking shoes. Most hill station activities involve walking on uneven terrain -- forest trails, viewpoint paths, and cobblestone streets. Avoid sandals for sightseeing.
  • Insect repellent. Hill stations surrounded by forests (Valparai, Wayanad, Coorg) have leeches during monsoon and mosquitoes year-round at certain altitudes. Carry repellent and wear covered footwear on forest walks.
  • Sunscreen. The UV intensity at altitude is higher than on the plains, even when it feels cool. Apply sunscreen for outdoor activities.
