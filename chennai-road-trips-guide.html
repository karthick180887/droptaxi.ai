Top 15 Road Trips from Chennai: Best Weekend Getaways by Car

Chennai is more than just a metropolitan city -- it is a launchpad for some of the most scenic, spiritual, and soul-stirring road trips in South India. Whether you want a quick weekend escape to the French Quarter of Pondicherry or a longer hill station retreat to Kodaikanal, the highways radiating from Chennai connect you to beaches, temples, mountains, and heritage towns within a few hours of driving.

This guide covers the 15 best road trips from Chennai, complete with distances, driving times, ideal seasons, must-see attractions, and practical tips for planning your journey. If you prefer to skip the self-drive hassle, every destination listed here is also available as a comfortable one-way taxi ride with DropTaxi.

1. Chennai to Pondicherry

Distance: 150 km | Drive Time: 2.5 to 3 hours | Best Season: October to March

Pondicherry, officially known as Puducherry, is the most popular weekend getaway from Chennai. The East Coast Road (ECR) that connects the two cities is itself one of the most scenic coastal highways in India, running alongside the Bay of Bengal with salt pans, fishing villages, and casuarina groves on either side.

What to see: The French Quarter (White Town) with its pastel colonial buildings and tree-lined boulevards. The Sri Aurobindo Ashram for spiritual reflection. Auroville, the experimental township dedicated to human unity. Promenade Beach for a sunrise stroll. Paradise Beach accessible by a short boat ride. Serenity Beach for surfing and watersports.

Why visit: Pondicherry offers an unusual blend of French colonial heritage, Tamil culture, spiritual retreat centers, and beach vibes -- all within a 3-hour drive from Chennai. The food scene alone, with its French bakeries, Tamil street food, and seafood restaurants, makes it worth the trip.

2. Chennai to Mahabalipuram

Distance: 58 km | Drive Time: 1 to 1.5 hours | Best Season: November to February

Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the closest major historical destination from Chennai. This ancient port city of the Pallava dynasty is home to some of the most remarkable rock-cut architecture in the world, carved directly from granite hillsides over 1,300 years ago.

What to see: Shore Temple, one of the oldest structural stone temples in South India, standing on the edge of the Bay of Bengal. Arjuna's Penance, the world's largest open-air bas-relief. The Five Rathas (Pancha Rathas), monolithic temples carved from single rocks. Krishna's Butterball, a massive balancing boulder. The Mahabalipuram beach for a relaxed evening. The Tiger Cave, a rock-cut shrine nearby.

Why visit: It is impossible to find a destination this historically significant this close to Chennai. Mahabalipuram works perfectly as a half-day trip, a full-day outing, or a stopover on the way to Pondicherry via ECR.

3. Chennai to Tiruvannamalai

Distance: 190 km | Drive Time: 3.5 to 4 hours | Best Season: November to February (Karthigai Deepam festival in Nov/Dec is spectacular)

Tiruvannamalai is one of the most important Shaivite pilgrimage centers in Tamil Nadu, dominated by the sacred Arunachala Hill and the massive Arunachaleswarar Temple. The town attracts both devout pilgrims performing the Girivalam (circumambulation of the hill) and spiritual seekers visiting the Ramana Maharshi Ashram.

What to see: Arunachaleswarar Temple, one of the Pancha Bhootha Sthalams representing fire. The Girivalam path, a 14 km walkway around Arunachala Hill lined with lingams and small temples. Sri Ramana Ashram (Ramanasramam). Virupaksha Cave where Ramana Maharshi meditated. Sathanur Dam, a scenic reservoir about 35 km away.

Why visit: Tiruvannamalai offers a deeply spiritual experience unlike any other destination near Chennai. The energy of Arunachala, the simplicity of ashram life, and the profound silence of the caves create a journey that is as much inward as it is outward.

4. Chennai to Vellore

Distance: 135 km | Drive Time: 2.5 to 3 hours | Best Season: October to March

Vellore is best known for the stunning Vellore Fort and the golden-hued Sripuram Golden Temple, but it also serves as an important medical hub with the renowned Christian Medical College (CMC). The drive from Chennai passes through the green landscapes of northern Tamil Nadu.

What to see: Vellore Fort, a 16th-century military fortification with a moat, church, mosque, and temple within its walls. Sripuram Golden Temple, a spiritual park covered in 1,500 kg of gold. Jalakandeswarar Temple inside the fort. Amirthi Zoological Park, about 25 km from the city. CMC campus with its colonial-era architecture.

Why visit: Vellore combines military history, religious splendor, and natural beauty in a compact area. It is also a convenient stopover for those heading to Tiruvannamalai or Yelagiri Hills.

5. Chennai to Kodaikanal

Distance: 525 km | Drive Time: 8 to 9 hours | Best Season: April to June, September to November

Known as the "Princess of Hill Stations," Kodaikanal sits at an altitude of 2,133 meters in the Palani Hills of the Western Ghats. While the drive from Chennai is long, the reward is one of the most beautiful and unspoiled hill stations in South India, built around a star-shaped lake and surrounded by dense shola forests and grasslands.

What to see: Kodaikanal Lake, a man-made star-shaped lake perfect for boating and cycling. Coaker's Walk, a paved pedestrian path with panoramic views of the plains below. Pillar Rocks, three granite pillars rising 400 feet from the ground. Bryant Park, a beautifully maintained botanical garden. Dolphin's Nose viewpoint. Pine forests and eucalyptus groves. Bear Shola Falls during monsoon. Berijam Lake for a forest drive (permit required).

Why visit: Kodaikanal has a charm that Ooty has lost to commercialization. The misty mornings, the quiet forest walks, the aroma of homemade chocolate from local shops, and the star-shaped lake create an experience of genuine hill station magic.

6. Chennai to Ooty

Distance: 560 km | Drive Time: 9 to 10 hours | Best Season: April to June, October to November

Ooty (Udhagamandalam) is the "Queen of Hill Stations" and the capital of the Nilgiris district. Established as a summer retreat by the British, Ooty retains its colonial character with botanical gardens, tea estates, and the iconic Nilgiri Mountain Railway (a UNESCO World Heritage railway).

What to see: Ooty Botanical Gardens, established in 1848 with over 650 species of plants. Ooty Lake for boating. Doddabetta Peak, the highest point in the Nilgiris at 2,637 meters. Rose Garden with 20,000 varieties. Tea Museum and tea estate walks. Pykara Falls and Pykara Lake. The Nilgiri Mountain Railway toy train from Mettupalayam to Ooty.

Why visit: Ooty is a complete hill station experience -- colonial heritage, nature walks, tea plantation tours, and cool weather year-round. The drive through the 36 hairpin bends of the Nilgiri Ghat road is itself a memorable experience.

7. Chennai to Coimbatore

Distance: 505 km | Drive Time: 7.5 to 8.5 hours | Best Season: Year-round (October to March is ideal)

Coimbatore is a major industrial city at the foothills of the Western Ghats, but its real appeal for road trippers lies in its position as a gateway to Ooty, Coonoor, Valparai, and other Nilgiri destinations. The city itself has grown into a vibrant food and culture hub.

What to see: Isha Yoga Center and Adiyogi Shiva statue (the world's largest bust sculpture). Marudamalai Temple perched on a hilltop. VOC Park and its zoo. Kovai Kondattam amusement park. The Siruvani waterfalls area. Use Coimbatore as a base to explore Ooty (88 km), Coonoor (68 km), Valparai (100 km), and Pollachi (40 km).

Why visit: Coimbatore offers excellent food (famous for its Kongunadu cuisine), comfortable stays at reasonable prices, and unmatched access to the Western Ghats. It is the most practical gateway city for exploring the Nilgiris.

8. Chennai to Madurai

Distance: 460 km | Drive Time: 7 to 8 hours | Best Season: October to March

Madurai is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and the cultural capital of Tamil Nadu. The entire city radiates outward from the towering Meenakshi Amman Temple, one of the most magnificent temple complexes ever built.

What to see: Meenakshi Amman Temple with its 14 colorful gopurams covered in thousands of painted stucco figures. Thirumalai Nayakkar Mahal, a 17th-century palace with massive pillars. Gandhi Memorial Museum. Alagar Kovil and Pazhamudhir Solai, two of the six abodes of Lord Murugan. Madurai's famous flower market (Muthupettai flower market). Night ceremony at Meenakshi Temple.

Why visit: No trip to Tamil Nadu is complete without experiencing Madurai. The Meenakshi Temple alone justifies the journey, but add in the city's 2,500-year history, its incredible street food, and its position as a base for Kodaikanal and Rameshwaram, and you have a destination of unmatched depth.

9. Chennai to Trichy (Tiruchirappalli)

Distance: 330 km | Drive Time: 5 to 6 hours | Best Season: October to March

Trichy is a city of monumental scale, home to the Rock Fort Temple perched on an 83-meter-high rock outcrop and the island temple complex of Srirangam, which is the largest functioning Hindu temple in the world by area.

What to see: Rock Fort Ucchi Pillayar Temple with panoramic views of the city and the Cauvery River. Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple at Srirangam, spread over 156 acres with 21 gopurams. Jambukeswarar Temple (one of the Pancha Bhootha Sthalams, representing water). St. Lourdes Church, a replica of the Basilica of Our Lady of Lourdes in France. Kallanai Dam, the oldest water-diversion structure in the world, built by the Chola king Karikalan.

Why visit: Trichy is a must-visit for anyone interested in Tamil temple architecture. Srirangam alone can take half a day to explore. The city also serves as a strategic midpoint for trips to Thanjavur, Kumbakonam, and Madurai.

10. Chennai to Rameshwaram

Distance: 570 km | Drive Time: 9 to 10 hours | Best Season: October to April

Rameshwaram is one of the four Char Dham pilgrimage sites and sits on Pamban Island, connected to mainland India by the iconic Pamban Bridge. The journey from Chennai takes you deep into southern Tamil Nadu, through increasingly dramatic landscapes until you cross the bridge over the shallow Palk Strait.

What to see: Ramanathaswamy Temple, famous for its 1,212-pillar corridor, the longest in any Hindu temple. The 22 theerthams (sacred wells) within the temple complex. Pamban Bridge, India's first sea bridge and an engineering marvel. Dhanushkodi, the ghost town at the tip of the island destroyed by the 1964 cyclone. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Memorial. Agnitheertham, the sacred sea-bathing spot.

Why visit: Rameshwaram is a deeply spiritual destination with a raw, untouched beauty that few other pilgrimage towns possess. The drive across Pamban Bridge, the sight of the temple corridors stretching into infinity, and the eerie ruins of Dhanushkodi create an unforgettable experience.

11. Chennai to Kanchipuram

Distance: 72 km | Drive Time: 1.5 to 2 hours | Best Season: October to March

Kanchipuram, one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism, was the capital of the Pallava dynasty and later the Chola dynasty. Known as the "City of Thousand Temples," it is also world-famous for its handwoven silk sarees. This is the ideal short day trip from Chennai for culture and history enthusiasts.

What to see: Ekambareswarar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva with a 3,500-year-old mango tree in its courtyard. Kailasanathar Temple, the oldest structure in Kanchipuram built in the 7th century. Kamakshi Amman Temple. Varadharaja Perumal Temple. Silk weaving centers and shops. Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary (about 30 km south).

Why visit: Kanchipuram packs more history per square kilometer than almost any other town in South India. The silk saree shopping alone draws visitors from across the country, and the temple architecture spans multiple dynasties and centuries.

12. Chennai to Yelagiri

Distance: 230 km | Drive Time: 4 to 4.5 hours | Best Season: Year-round (October to February is ideal)

Yelagiri is a small, quiet hill station in the Vellore district, sitting at about 940 meters above sea level. It is far less crowded than Ooty or Kodaikanal and offers a peaceful retreat with pleasant weather, rose gardens, and panoramic views of the surrounding plains.

What to see: Yelagiri Lake (Punganoor Lake) for boating. Nature Park and rose garden. Swamimalai Hills for trekking. Jalagamparai Waterfalls (seasonal). Paragliding at Yelagiri Adventure Zone. Nilavoor Lake for a quiet picnic. The 14 hairpin bends driving up from Jolarpet.

Why visit: Yelagiri is the perfect antidote to overcrowded hill stations. If you want crisp air, quiet walks, and no traffic jams, this under-the-radar destination delivers exactly that. The shorter driving distance compared to Ooty or Kodaikanal makes it ideal for a quick 2-day weekend trip.

13. Chennai to Yercaud

Distance: 365 km | Drive Time: 5.5 to 6.5 hours | Best Season: Year-round (September to May is best)

Yercaud is a compact hill station in the Shevaroy Hills of the Eastern Ghats, perched at about 1,515 meters above sea level. Often called the "Jewel of the South" or "Poor Man's Ooty," Yercaud offers coffee plantations, a pleasant lake, and remarkably fewer crowds than its western counterpart.

What to see: Yercaud Lake (Emerald Lake) for boating. Lady's Seat viewpoint with panoramic views of the Salem plains. Kiliyur Falls, a 90-meter cascade accessible by a trek. Bear's Cave (Karadu Cave). Pagoda Point and Grange viewpoint. Coffee and spice plantations. Shevaroy Temple at the highest point.

Why visit: Yercaud is one of the most affordable and accessible hill stations in Tamil Nadu. The 20 hairpin bends driving up from Salem are scenic without being terrifying, and the town itself has a laid-back, village-like atmosphere that is increasingly rare.

14. Chennai to Coonoor

Distance: 540 km | Drive Time: 8.5 to 9.5 hours | Best Season: October to March

Coonoor is the second-largest hill station in the Nilgiris after Ooty, but it feels like a different world entirely. While Ooty has become a busy tourist hub, Coonoor retains a quieter, more authentic character with its tea estates, colonial bungalows, and misty valleys.

What to see: Sim's Park, a 12-hectare botanical garden with over 1,000 plant species. Dolphin's Nose viewpoint for spectacular views of the Catherine Falls and Ketti Valley. Lamb's Rock viewpoint. Hidden Valley and Tiger Hill. Tea plantations -- Coonoor produces some of the finest Nilgiri tea. Droog Fort ruins for a short trek. The toy train ride between Coonoor and Ooty.

Why visit: Coonoor offers the Nilgiri experience without the Ooty crowds. The tea estates are more accessible, the viewpoints are less congested, and the town has excellent homestays and boutique properties. Food lovers will find some of the best bakeries and cafes in the Nilgiris here.

15. Chennai to Kumbakonam

Distance: 295 km | Drive Time: 5 to 5.5 hours | Best Season: October to March (Mahamaham festival every 12 years)

Kumbakonam is the temple town of the Cauvery Delta, with over 188 temples within its municipal limits. It is also the heart of the Chola temple circuit and the ideal base for visiting the UNESCO-listed Brihadeeswarar temples in Thanjavur and Darasuram.

What to see: Adi Kumbeswarar Temple, the central temple of the town. Sarangapani Temple, one of the 108 Divya Desams. Nageswaran Temple with exquisite Chola-era frescoes. Mahamaham Tank, the sacred tank where the 12-yearly festival occurs. Airavatesvara Temple at Darasuram (UNESCO World Heritage Site), just 3 km away. Swamimalai and Thiruvidaimarudur temples nearby. Degree Coffee -- Kumbakonam is the filter coffee capital of Tamil Nadu.

Why visit: Kumbakonam is a living museum of Chola-era temple art and architecture. The town retains an old-world charm with its agraharam streets (traditional Brahmin quarters), brass lamp shops, and the aroma of filter coffee wafting from every corner. Combined with nearby Thanjavur, it forms the richest temple-touring experience in India.

Tips for Road Trips from Chennai

  • Start early. Leave Chennai before 5:30 AM to avoid the notorious city traffic and highway truck traffic. Most long-distance destinations are best reached by noon.
  • Fuel up before you leave. Fill your tank in the city. While petrol bunks are available on highways, they can be crowded during peak hours and weekends.
  • Carry cash for tolls. The National Highways from Chennai have multiple toll plazas. FASTag works on most, but keep cash as backup.
  • Avoid night driving on ghat roads. Routes to Ooty, Kodaikanal, Coonoor, Yercaud, and Yelagiri involve hairpin bends that are dangerous in the dark, especially during monsoon.
  • Consider a one-way taxi. For long drives (Kodaikanal, Ooty, Rameshwaram), taking a one-way taxi lets you relax, enjoy the scenery, and arrive refreshed. You only pay for the one-way distance -- no return fare.
